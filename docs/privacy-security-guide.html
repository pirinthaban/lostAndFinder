Our Commitment to Your Safety

At FindBack, trust and security are paramount. This guide explains how we protect your data and what you can do to stay safe.

üîê How We Protect Your Data

Encryption

  • Messages: End-to-end encryption (AES-256)
  • Data Transfer: TLS 1.3 / HTTPS
  • Storage: Firebase encryption at rest
  • Passwords: Hashed (never stored in plain text)

Privacy Features

  • Auto-Blur: Automatically blurs NIC numbers, faces, and sensitive data
  • Location Fuzzing: Shows approximate area, not exact GPS
  • Phone Masking: Your number stays private until you share it
  • No Data Selling: We never sell your information

‚úÖ Security Best Practices for Users

üîí Protect Your Account

  • Use a strong password (if using email login)
  • Enable biometric authentication (Face ID / Fingerprint)
  • Never share your OTP codes with anyone
  • Report suspicious accounts immediately

üì∏ Post Items Safely

  • Review photos before posting (check for private info in background)
  • Use approximate location, not your exact address
  • Don't include full NIC/passport numbers in description
  • Verify claimants thoroughly before arranging meetup

üí¨ Communication Safety

  • Keep conversations in-app (messages are encrypted)
  • Never send payment for returning items
  • Report scams and fake claims immediately
  • Block abusive users
  • Trust your instincts

ü§ù Safe Meetups

  • Always meet in public places (malls, cafes, police stations)
  • Bring a friend if possible
  • Meet during daylight hours
  • Verify item and ownership proof before handover
  • Never go to someone's home alone

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I use FindBack anonymously?
A: No, phone verification is required to prevent fraud and ensure accountability.

Q: Who can see my phone number?
A: Only you, until you choose to share it in chat with specific users.

Q: How long are deleted items kept?
A: 30 days in case of accidental deletion, then permanently removed.

Q: Can police access my data?
A: Only with a valid legal warrant or court order.

Q: Is my location tracked continuously?
A: No, location is only used when posting items or searching nearby.
